About Aleena

Aleena Anabelly A is a legal practitioner, cultural advocate, and author whose work focuses on the intersection of law, culture, and social justice. She holds a BBA LL.B. (Hons) degree from the School of Law, CHRIST University, Bengaluru, where she ranked first in her batch.

Education & Recognition

Aleena's academic background spans legal studies, political philosophy, and cultural studies. She is an alumna of the University of Oxford's Department of Continuing Education and a recipient of the Goolam E. Vahanvati Memorial Merit Scholarship. Her research paper on inequality in India was recognized by the United Nations 75 Dialogue Creation Program, where she was named "Ambassador for Equality."

Professional Work

As a published author, Aleena's book "Samsara: The Revived Glory of Indian Art Forms" was published under the PM YUVA mentorship program and has been showcased at international book fairs. As a lawyer practicing before the High Court of Kerala, she represents indigenous communities in intellectual property rights litigation, advocating for the recognition of collective artistic ownership.

Impact & Vision

Born in Kannur, Kerala, Aleena developed a deep connection to traditional art forms that shaped her advocacy work. Her contributions have been recognized through an invitation to meet the President of India and her selection for the Gandhi-King Scholarly Exchange Initiative by the U.S. Department of State. Through her legal practice and writing, she works to protect traditional cultural expressions and advocates for legal frameworks that honor India's diverse artistic legacy.
